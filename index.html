Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about emergency plumbing service in Richardson or Plano? Here are answers to the questions we hear most often from homeowners and property managers.

If you see active water spraying, flowing, or pooling, the first step is to shut off the water supply to stop or slow the damage. Most homes have a main shutoff valve near the street, meter, garage, or utility area. If it is safe to do so, turn this valve clockwise to shut off water to the entire house.

Once the water is off, call our emergency line so we can dispatch a technician to your Richardson or Plano address. If you are not sure where your shutoff is or how to operate it, call us immediately—we can often guide you over the phone or advise you on the safest next step while help is on the way.

Response times vary depending on call volume and traffic, but for most emergencies in Richardson, Plano, and the surrounding neighborhoods, we aim to be on-site within 60–90 minutes. During periods of unusually high demand, we will always give you an honest estimated arrival time and keep you updated if anything changes.

If your situation is especially urgent—for example, a rapidly spreading leak or sewage backup—let our dispatcher know so we can prioritize your call and offer guidance on minimizing damage while you wait.

Because emergency service requires dedicated on-call staff and rapid response, there may be different rates for after-hours, weekend, or holiday calls compared to standard weekday appointments. That said, we are committed to transparent, upfront pricing with no surprises once the work is done.

Before any work begins, your technician will assess the situation, explain your options, and provide clear pricing so you can make an informed decision. If additional issues are discovered during the repair, we will always discuss them with you and get your approval before moving forward.

While we are not an insurance company and cannot guarantee coverage, we regularly work with homeowners who are filing claims for water damage. We can provide detailed invoices, descriptions of the cause of the problem, and photos of the affected plumbing to support your conversations with your insurance provider.

If you believe your situation may involve a claim, let us know when you call. We can focus on stopping further damage quickly, document our findings carefully, and help you understand which parts of the repair are most likely to be relevant to your insurer.

We handle a wide range of urgent issues for homes and small businesses in Richardson and Plano, including burst or leaking pipes, overflowing or clogged toilets, backed-up drains and main sewer lines, sudden loss of water pressure, broken shutoff valves, leaking or failed water heaters, and more.

If you are not sure whether your situation counts as an "emergency," give us a call anyway. We can help you assess the risk over the phone and either dispatch an emergency technician or schedule a convenient same- or next-day appointment if the issue is less urgent.
